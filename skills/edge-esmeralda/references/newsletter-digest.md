WHAT IS EDGE TOMORROW?

Many of us want our children to experience what village life looks like: a place where people of all ages collaborate, learn from one another, and create meaningful things together. Edge Tomorrow is an experiment in intergenerational creativity that makes this real. It draws on Reggio Emilia’s model of place-based, project-centered learning and Montessori’s prepared environment focused on autonomy and interdependence.

Kids join Edge Esmeralda as fellow citizens, stakeholders, and builders. They lead the research, define the projects, and shape the culture of the village alongside adults who are there to collaborate, support, and learn with them. The goal is to create a world where the people who will be our leaders, healers, and artists of tomorrow get to feel what it feels like to imagine, collaborate, and bring something real to life.

Each week is anchored by a theme-question that sparks inquiry and project development. Generative themes we’re considering include “welcoming,” “cozy places and dreaming,” and “neighborhoods of the future.” Themes will adapt to each week’s cohort, and children are central to the conception, design and creation of the projects.

We’re calling it a residency — instead of camp or child care — to remind ourselves that what happens at Edge Tomorrow connects with and contributes to the whole village.


THE WEEKLY STRUCTURE

 * Monday: Discovery and exploration

 * Tuesday: Experiments and project definition

 * Wednesday & Thursday: Building

 * Friday: Final touches, community showcase, project retro

Creative residency hours run Monday–Friday, 9am–4pm. Each day offers a mix of structured collaboration and open play, maximizing child agency.


WHO IS IT FOR?

Kids (ages 7–12): Independent, creative children who will lead the design and making. This is who the residency is built around.

Adults: Parents, aunties, uncles, and other Edge attendees who want to work alongside kids on ambitious projects. You bring your skills and passions; the kids bring the vision.

Teens: Teens, especially creators and makers who are open to stepping into leadership and mentoring roles with younger kids, want to help them bring to life projects.

Elders: Grandmas, grandpas, nanas and beyond, join us to hold space, share stories, build hands-on with us, and bring the richness of your life experience to the intergenerational mix.


PRICING

Full month (4 weeks): $1,680 per child

Single week: $560 per child

Prices will increase as we get closer to the event. We encourage planning early so we can get to know your family and help create the best experience.


THE APPROACH

We aspire to create the DNA for Esmeralda to become a City of Children, as visionary thinker Francesco Tonucci first conceived. In addition to high-agency traditions like Reggio Emilia and Montessori, Edge Tomorrow is informed by child-led governance models like Children’s Parliaments and sociocracy for kids.

The program is part of a broader exploration into how temporary village labs can bring children, teens, and adults together around meaningful creative projects. To learn more you are welcome to join me at The Center for Numinous Worldbuilding on Substack, where I write about intergenerational learning, attention health, and village experiments.


SCREEN AND TECH CULTURE

Edge Tomorrow is a hands-on creative environment. We think of technology as a tool for making, not passive entertainment. If screens are used, they serve the project: documenting, recording, designing, researching. Rest time means books, drawing, outdoor play, and screen-free games. We recommend children leave phones, tablets, and gaming devices at home or with parents during studio hours.

Over the past two years, this program ran as the Edge City Fellowship, supporting four cohorts of builders. Starting in 2026, we’re renaming it the Inflection Fellowship. The reason is simple: this fellowship and our Inflection Grants program share a core belief that small, well-timed interventions during a young builder’s formative years can become genuine inflection points in their trajectory. The unified name reflects that shared philosophy, which we and Long Journey are thrilled to push forward.

----------------------------------------

The Inflection Fellowship is a month-long in-person program that empowers 12 exceptional builders under the age of 25 per year who are dedicated to accelerating human flourishing across frontier tech, science, and society. It is organized by Edge City and Long Journey.

The fellowship features a full-ride to a monthly Edge City flagship event in 2026. The experience involves living amongst hundreds of multidisciplinary builders, thinkers, creatives, and industry leaders. The first cohort will participate in Edge Esmerelda in California; the second cohort will participate in Edge City India in Q4 2026.

Who it is for: high-agency builders under 25 years old who are working on a magically weird project, problem space, or kernel of an idea that would benefit from the multidisciplinary setting of an Edge City.

You don’t have to already be thinking about building a company around your idea; previous fellows have used this time to enable locals to create a biomechanical artwork show, solve an “unsolvable” maths theorem, and work on the politics of AGI.

## Democratizing Science at Edge City 

**Date**: Tue, 17 Feb 2026 18:07:35 GMT | **Author**: Katherine Jones
**Link**: https://edgeesmeralda2026.substack.com/p/democratizing-science-at-edge-city

At Edge City, our popup villages function as living labs. Across our gatherings, residents run experiments in everything from new governance models, to community sleep data aggregation, to longevity protocols and consciousness research. The missing piece is often the same: how do you actually measure whether something works?

That’s why we’re excited to announce a partnership with Alethios, a platform that makes it easy for anyone to design and run structured, real-world studies. Through this collaboration, all Edge City participants - residents, fellows, and curious attendees - will get free access to the Alethios Study Planner to design and operate studies at our villages as well as on-the-ground support to transform ideas into research.

Thanks for reading Edge Esmeralda 2026! Subscribe for free to receive new posts and support my work.




WHY THIS MATTERS

When someone runs a week-long breathwork experiment, tests a new sleep protocol, or explores the effects of a community practice on wellbeing, we want them to be able to collect real data and draw meaningful conclusions. Until now, most of that knowledge has stayed at the level of anecdotes.



Alethios makes this accessible. You don’t need a lab, an institutional affiliation, or an enterprise budget. You can design a study, set up a participant-facing app (mobile and web-based), and integrate data from surveys, wearables, cognitive assessments, and optional biomarker collection - all in one platform. Their team also supports ethics committee (IRB) submissions and compliance in the United States.


WHAT THIS LOOKS LIKE IN PRACTICE

Imagine you’re at one of our popup villages and you want to test whether a daily cold plunge routine affects cognitive performance over two weeks. Or whether a particular co-living arrangement leads to higher self-reported wellbeing. Or whether a meditation protocol changes biomarkers in a measurable way.

With Alethios, you can design that study in days, not months. You can recruit participants from the village, set up clear schedules and tasks for them, and collect high-quality data - all while fitting naturally into the rhythms of community life.

This is the kind of infrastructure that lets us move from “I think this worked for me” to “here’s what the data shows, and here’s who it worked for”.




A NATURAL FIT

Alethios’ mission to democratize research aligns closely with how we think about experimentation. Our villages bring together people with questions worth investigating with rigor, and now they have a real tool to do it. THREE TYPES OF PROGRAMMING (AND HOW YOU CAN GET INVOLVED)

We’ve organized this year’s programming into three formats, each serving a different purpose. We’ll share more on each in the coming weeks, but here’s the framework.


TRACKS

Tracks are about showcasing ideas. A track is a day or week of thematic programming - talks, workshops, salons, unconferences - led by a person or small team with knowledge and a strong network in their domain. Tracks are the most visible part of Edge Esmeralda. They attract experts, create entry points for the whole village, and most sessions are open to all.



Examples from EE25:

 * Vital Futures: Daily labs, blood testing, and evening salons exploring metabolic health and biotech innovation.

 * Consciousness Week: Bridged neuroscience, AI, and contemplative traditions, from vasocomputation theory to cross-tradition mappings of enlightenment.

 * Cities of Tomorrow: Explored urban design, community infrastructure, and civic technology. Featured a fireside with Audrey Tang on digital democracy.

 * Crypto Academic Camp: Leading crypto researchers in deep, off-record conversation on mechanism design, policy, and foundational problems in DeFi.


RESIDENCIES

Residencies are about building and creating. A residency brings together a cohort of 10–20 builders: founders, researchers, developers, creators - around a shared thesis. They can last a couple of weeks or the whole month.

The focus is on shipping: projects launched, papers written, teams formed. Many residencies also run a track during their time, spending most of the month heads-down and then hosting a week of programming to share their work with the village.

Past examples:

 * Long Journey Residency (EE 2025): 14 builders working across synthetic biology, neurotech, AI infrastructure, and more. Residents used the village as a testbed for frontier projects.

 * d/acc Residency (Edge City Patagonia 2025): Residents worked on pathogen detection, DAO governance, onchain collective intelligence, privacy-preserving financial proofs, and more.

 * World Builder Residency (EE 2025): Developers and designers building apps using World ID, World Chain, and MiniKit. Three projects received bounties for outstanding work.

 * Agartha House (Edge City Patagonia 2025): A creativity incubator exploring benevolent AI agents, new startups, and solarpunk community models.


EXPERIMENTS

Experiments are about testing. When you have hundreds of curious, high-trust people in one place willing to try new tools, share data, and participate in novel protocols, you get a rare chance to run applied research in the real world.

Experiments range from piloting new products with a live user base to prototyping social protocols and collecting data that would be hard to gather elsewhere.

Past examples:

 * Constellation (EE 2025): Piloted a daily neural data collection protocol — participants wore neural caps for an hour a day to help train a foundation model of brain state.

 * Fulcra Dynamics (EE 2025): Aggregated wearable data into a village-wide wellness dashboard showing how people were moving, sleeping, and connecting.

 * RadicalxChange Community Currency (EE 2024 & Edge City Lanna): Piloted a community currency experiment, unlocking new ideas for decentralized economic models.

 * Off-Grid Solar Datacenter (EE 2025): A solar-powered, battery-backed system running on-site to support GPU-intensive projects — testing new approaches to sustainable, decentralized compute.

 * Dynamic Health (Edge Patagonia 2025): A multimodal health data experiment, combining biometric tracking with resilience protocols (retro here).



These categories overlap, and that’s by design. A residency might run a track. A track might spawn an experiment. An experiment might be run by a residency.

The d/acc Residency in Patagonia ran weekly demos and contributed programming throughout the month. Vital Futures kicked off live blood testing and biometric tracking that became ongoing experiments. The boundaries between formats are porous, and the best programs tend to cross them.
